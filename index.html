Military Training

I officially enlisted in July 2021. Just hours after, basic training began. It was intense and unfamiliar, but I pushed through and completed it with pride.

Technical Training Course

Assigned as a Network Administrator — a technical role for which I had no prior experience — I faced a steep learning curve. Transitioning from a small high school class of 8 to a military course with 30 students and rigorous exams was overwhelming. Yet, with support from peers and commanders, I thrived and graduated as an outstanding soldier.

Active Duty

In active service, I maintained IT infrastructure: handling hardware malfunctions, managing networks and domains. Later, I transitioned into the role of Multimedia Manager — creating and editing content for my unit. This blend of tech and creativity allowed me to thrive, empowered by the trust of my commanders. Toward the end, I wished to continue but no suitable roles were open. Leaving was devastating.

Military Memories

✦ Who I Am

Hi, I'm Yaeer. I proudly served in the IDF, but the path wasn’t easy. Though I received an autism-related exemption in 2019, something inside me knew I had more to give. I discovered the Titkadmu Program, offering people like me an opportunity to serve meaningfully.

In July 2021, I enlisted, completed training, and began my journey as a Network Administrator. Soon, I also took on the role of Multimedia Manager, aligning with my graphic design background from high school. These roles helped me blend my passions for tech and creativity — and gave me a sense of purpose.

✦ Saying Goodbye

As my service concluded, I longed to stay. I had finally found where I belonged. But I was discharged. That day remains one of the most difficult of my life. I left the gates of the base in tears.

✦ My Mission Continues

I’m currently training to become a QA and Automation Officer, learning software testing and Python automation. I aim to return — whether through reserves or a specialized officer program.

This is my journey. This is my mission. I will return stronger.
